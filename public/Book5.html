DAVID BALDACCI

"Travis Devine, an ex-Army Ranger turned entry-level analyst at a prestigious investment firm, finds his mundane routine shattered when his former girlfriend and coworker, Sara Ewes, is found dead. Threatened with exposure of his past military secrets, Devine is coerced into investigating his firm's clandestine activities. As he delves deeper, Devine uncovers a treacherous conspiracy and becomes a target himself. With the help of his roommates and allies, Devine navigates a web of deceit and danger, ultimately unraveling a shocking truth that ties together a series of crimes. Through twists and turns, Devine confronts his past and brings closure to a dark chapter of his life."
"The Whole Truth" by David Baldacci, arms contractor Nicolas Creel and PM guru Dick Pender conspire to manipulate global perceptions and incite fear to profit from defense contracts. Super-secret spy Shaw and ex-alcoholic journalist Katie James reluctantly team up to thwart their nefarious plans. However, the plot suffers from inconsistencies, one-dimensional characters, and unrealistic scenarios, ultimately failing to deliver a compelling thriller experience."
"Long Shadows" by David Baldacci, FBI agent Amos Decker faces personal and professional challenges as he investigates the mysterious suicide of a colleague and the murder of a judge in Florida. Partnered with a new colleague, Frederica White, Decker delves into the complexities of the cases, uncovering connections to past presidential campaigns and high-level power struggles. As the investigation deepens, Decker navigates through a web of secrets and deceit, ultimately confronting his own vulnerabilities and the shifting dynamics of his relationships. With a blend of multiple cases, intricate plot twists, and evolving character dynamics, "Long Shadows" delivers a compelling and suspenseful addition to the Memory Man series."
"A Calamity of Souls" by David Baldacci, set in 1968 southern Virginia, racial tensions escalate as white lawyer Jack Lee and Black lawyer Desiree DuBose team up to defend a Black man, Jerome Washington, wrongly accused of murdering a white couple. Facing prejudice and injustice in the legal system, Lee and DuBose navigate a hostile environment while uncovering lies and fighting for justice. As they confront the deep-rooted racism and political machinations of the era, they strive to ensure a fair trial and uncover the truth, ultimately revealing the real killer and confronting the systemic biases that permeate society. Through compelling characters and realistic portrayal of the era, Baldacci highlights the struggle for equality and the enduring impact of racial injustice."
"In David Baldacci's "Zero Day," Army Warrant Officer John Puller is tasked with investigating the brutal murder of Colonel Matthew Reynolds and his family in Drake, West Virginia. Puller, accompanied by local officer Samantha Cole, delves into the case and uncovers a complex web of deception and conspiracy. As Puller digs deeper, he discovers ties to a mine explosion, financial irregularities, and even potential nuclear terrorism. With the help of his incarcerated brother, Robert, Puller races against time to disarm a nuclear bomb and apprehend those responsible. Along the way, Puller confronts corrupt officials and uncovers shocking truths about the true perpetrators of the crime. Despite facing immense danger and personal loss, Puller remains determined to seek justice and protect innocent lives."
"Dream Town" by David Baldacci, Aloysius Archer, a former soldier turned private investigator, finds himself embroiled in a dangerous case in Los Angeles. While visiting his friend Liberty Callahan, Archer is approached by screenwriter Eleanor Lamb, who fears for her life. Before Archer can officially take on her case, Eleanor disappears, leaving behind a dead body in her home. Determined to find both Eleanor and the murderer, Archer delves into the dark underbelly of Hollywood, encountering mobsters, drug smugglers, and corrupt officials along the way. With the help of Liberty and his partner Willie Dash, Archer navigates the treacherous streets of LA, risking his own life to uncover the truth behind Eleanor's disappearance and the sinister forces at play. As the investigation unfolds, Archer confronts his own mortality and makes crucial decisions about his future. With Baldacci's trademark blend of suspense and character development"
